# Product

## Register

product

## Users
AI developers, media creators, and technical builders constructing automated AI media workflows. They need direct, visual, high-density feedback on their DAG execution, status, and credit consumption, with an emphasis on debugging and optimizing workflows in real time.

## Product Purpose
A DAG-based visual builder and runner for multi-node AI pipelines. Thinkly provides visual execution tracing, live status streaming, real-time resource control via a credit ledger, and a public REST/MCP API.

## Brand Personality
Precise, developer-focused, premium. Heavily inspired by iOS, Apple, and Emil Kowalski design engineering principles. It features a technical HUD console aesthetic, high contrast, typography-focused hierarchy, and cinematic/fluid motion.

## Anti-references
- Symmetrical, boring card grids without visual rhythm.
- Saturated gray-on-gray palettes that look washed out.
- Decorative gradient text, sketches, or wavy doodles.
- Side-stripe borders and harsh, dark drop shadows.
- Generic low-density layout clichés (like metric-hero blocks with wide spacing and tiny tracked eyebrows on every section).

## Design Principles
1. **Tactile Haptic Feedback:** Every click, press, or hover should produce an organic, physical response (scale-down press states, diagonal spring translations, and kinetic tension).
2. **Double-Bezel Nested Architecture (Doppelrand):** Content containers are framed like physical machined hardware shells, concentric and deeply structured.
3. **HUD High-Density Monospace Precision:** Short IDs, status capsules, and credit metrics are rendered with typographic precision, avoiding generic placeholders and prioritizing scannability.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Contrast ratios >= 4.5:1 for all text.
- Fallback animations (e.g., crossfading or instant transitions) when `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` is enabled.
- Distinct color and shape indicators for execution states (e.g. blue for running, emerald for success, red for error, zinc for idle).
